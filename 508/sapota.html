Sapota (Achras zapota) commonly known as chiku is mainly cultivated in India for its fruit value, while in South-East Mexico, Guatemala and other countries it is commercially grown for the production of chickle which is a gum like substance obtained from latex and is mainly used for preparation of chewing gum.The area under sapota cultivation has increased from 27 thousand ha. in 1991-92 to 52 thousand ha. in 2001-02 and production from 3.96 lakh tonnes to 5.94 lakh tones. Sapota is mostly grown in the states of Gujarat, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh and Kerala.
