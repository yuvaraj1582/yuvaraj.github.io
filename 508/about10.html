1.What is the Definition of Horticulture?

Horticulture is the branch of agriculture concerned with intensively cultured plants directly used by man for food, medicinal purposes and aesthetic gratification.

2.Who is the father of horticulture?

Being the doyen of horticulture, Dr. Chadha is aptly referred to as the 'Father of Modern Horticulture'.

3.What are the 4 types of Horticulture?

The four different branches in horticulture are pomology, olericulture, floriculture, and landscape horticulture.

4.What are the uses of Horticulture?

Horticulture, from Latin hortus meaning garden and colere meaning to cultivate, focuses on the use of small plots, in contrast to agronomy which involves intensive crop farming and large-scale field crop production of grains and forages or forestry involving forest trees and products derived from them.

5.What are the products of horticulture?

Horticultural crops include fruits, vegetables, medicinal, aromatic, and ornamental plants. These crops are important dietary nutritional components and sources of medicines and aroma along with significant esthetic values for human beings.

6.How does horticulture benefit farmers?

Horticulture enriches diets, Growing fruits and vegetables provide critical nutrients for a balanced diet.

7.What is difference between agriculture and horticulture?

While agriculture deals with all aspects of crop cultivation, horticulture deals with vegetables, trees, flowers, turf, shrubs, fruits and nuts.

8.Who is the largest producer of horticulture?

China. China tops the list by being the world's largest fruit and vegetable producer.

9.What are the factors affecting the growth of horticulture?

Light intensity and duration are important for crop growth and development. Low light causes plants to be spindly, small leaves, bud blades, poor pollination and poor fruit quality. Plants differ in light requirements. An example was given for horticultural crops

10.Why is horticulture the best?

A Horticulturist works to improve crop yield, quality, nutritional value, and resistance to insects, diseases, and environmental stresses.

11.What are the advantages of horticulture?

Horticulture increases incomes, Farmers growing high-value crops, such as fruits, vegetables, flowers or herbs, consistently earn more than those growing other commodities. It can be an engine for agricultural and economic diversification.
